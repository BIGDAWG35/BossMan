# Football Squares Learning

## Overview
Football squares (a.k.a. Super Bowl pools or 50/50 squares) - pool where a grid determines winners based on final digits of each team's score at end of each period.

---

## Square Formats

### 1. 10-by-10 Grid (Standard)
- 100 squares total
- Rows = Team A, Columns = Team B
- Digits 0-9 assigned to both axes after squares sold
- Payouts per period (quarter + final)

### 2. Smaller Formats
- 4x4 (16 squares) or 5x5 (25 squares)
- For smaller groups, casual pools
- Digits assigned differently (reuse subset)

### 3. 50/50 Board Style
- Single period payout
- Final score only

### 4. Multi-Period Boards
- Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4, Final
- Separate prize per period

### 5. Per-Quarter + Final Hybrids
- Rewards for quarter endings AND final score

---

## How to Bet & Assign Numbers

1. **Sell squares** - Fixed price ($1, $5, $10 per square)
2. **Assign teams** - Team A on rows, Team B on columns (random or chosen)
3. **Label digits** - After all squares filled, randomly assign 0-9 to axes
4. **Track scores** - At end of each period, get last digit of each team's score
5. **Find winner** - Intersection of digits = winner
6. **Payout** - Per pre-set distribution

---

## House Rules & Tips

### Clear Rules Document
- Price per square
- Number assignment method
- Payout per period
- Who handles funds
- Deadline

### Fair Number Assignment
- Use random draw after squares sold
- Prevents bias

### Guaranteed Payouts
- Set aside "house cut" or donation first

### Handling Ties
- If 17-17, decide tiebreaker or rollover

### Special Squares
- Double payout for certain digits

### Communication
- Share digital board
- Update after each quarter

---

## Step-by-Step Setup (DIY)

1. Decide format (10x10, 5x5, etc.)
2. Create grid with team labels
3. Collect entries + funds
4. Record buyer names per square
5. Randomly assign digits 0-9 to axes
6. Publish mapping
7. Track periods, compute last digits
8. Find intersection, payout
9. Confirm with winners
10. Post-event: Return funds or donate

## Basketball Squares & Betting

### Basketball Squares
Similar to football but uses basketball scores. Key differences:
- Periods = Quarters (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)
- Also halves in college basketball
- Same 10x10 grid concept

### Variations
- 5x5 or 8x8 grids (smaller)
- Payouts per quarter and/or half
- Digital versions for virtual gatherings

### Other Friendly Bets

#### Pick'em Pool
- Pick winner of each game
- Points for correct picks

#### Margin of Victory
- Guess final score or margin
- Closest wins

#### Parlay Challenges
- Small set of games
- All picks must be correct

#### Prop Games
- Player stats: points, rebounds, assists
- Team stats within a game

#### Survivor/Elimination Pools
- Pick one team to win each week
- Win = advance, lose = out

#### Scoring Pools
- Points for correct picks
- Tie-breakers: total points, final score guess

### Overtime Handling
- Updated digits
- Or carries over to next period

## Soccer (Football) Squares

### Similar to Other Sports
- 10x10 grid
- Based on score digits
- Periods = Halves (soccer has 2 halves, not quarters)

### Scoring in Sheets
- Use =RAND() to shuffle digits
- Small script for assignment

### Safety & Legality
- Keep stakes small and friendly
- Non-monetary prizes or low entry
- Check workplace policies on gambling

### Trusted Resources
- FIFA.com - Competition formats
- UEFA.com - European football regulations
- The Guardian - Soccer formats/articles
- American Gaming Association - Responsible play
- YouTube - Search "soccer pool rules"

- Entry: $1 per square
- Digits: Random after sale

**Example Prizes:**
